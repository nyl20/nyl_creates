Mindstate Ideation Hackathon: Experience and Takeaways

What is the MindState Ideation Hackathon?

The MindState Ideation Hackathon is a week long ideation sprint coordinated by MindState, where students from different schools collaborate to ideate and pitch a product in a competition. For this hackathon, MindState paired with BlueWhite, an innovative Ag Tech company, for students from Cornell Tech, Shenkar Institute, and UDEM to work with and ideate in the Ag Tech space. Four topics - Labor Forces, Environmental Resilience, Mental Health, Autonomous Fleet Management - were established and a group of students were assigned (based on student preference) to each topic, with each group having three students from Cornell Tech and 3 designers from both Shenkar and UDEM, as well as a mentor from BlueWhite. The groups work with each other in the month leading up to the week long sprint held in the Bay Area where they meet each other in person for the first time. It's an exciting opportunity to learn more about how to develop a product in a short amount of time with the guidance of an expert at the paired company.

Month Long Preparation

About a month before the trip to the Bay Area, I got the notification that I was placed in the Environmental Resilience group (my first choice!). I got to meet my fellow Cornell Tech teammates in person at our first meeting, while the students from Shenkar and UDEM and our mentor from BlueWhite connected with us from Zoom. The first meeting informed us of who our teammates and mentor would be, as well as a workshop with guest speakers who spoke about important factors to think about during the ideation process. We came up with preliminary ideas and honed in on the specific problems that we could address within the environmental resilience space.
