User Interfaces in Figma

Figma is a well-developed tool for creating designs in 2D that I have gained experience in through classes like HCI and Design. I have designed interfaces for multiple screen sizes including watches, mobile screens, and desktops. The images to the right are part of an interface I designed for an app that helps users find trails near them and track their activity. Other projects in Figma include initial designs for an app called SquashScore and a prototype for a social media based app.

3D Model of Gogo Technique

Virtual reality is getting increasingly popular and takes on a whole new dimension to design. Taking a course on 3D User Interfaces, I have been learning the fundamentals of designing for 3D and creating scenes in 3D. The video example here is my model of the gogo technique for selection and manipulation in the paper by Poupyrev et al.

Crochet Tech

Perhaps my most passionate projects are those done through crochet. Having learned crochet in college, I have quickly grown my skills and have recently combined crochet and science. At the Cornell Tech Art Collective, I crocheted electrical wire with yarn and was able to connect an LED light to the crochet flower (left image). I hope to explore more applications of tech in crochet and make interesting designs. Additionally, I have crocheted an art piece demonstrating the symbiotic relationship of plants and fungi (right image) that was displayed in the library at Cornell University.
